## Initial Notes

VB-002 (shaderDenormPreserveFloat64 optional on Vulkan) means subnormals may be flushed to zero on some hardware. The adversarial review says the 1-ULP claim is for normal inputs only. But: what EXACTLY breaks when a subnormal appears mid-computation, not just as input/output? For exp: if the range-reduction residual r is subnormal (near a Cody-Waite exact input), flushing r to zero gives exp(n*LN2) instead of exp(n*LN2 + r) — potentially 0.5 ULP error on the result. Map all Phase 1 functions' sensitivity to mid-computation subnormals.
