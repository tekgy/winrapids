## Initial Notes

Standing methodology observation: naturalist's output rhythm should vary with the day's phase. Day-one rhythm (trek opening, nothing in place): observe, ask, bring structural observations when slots are being named. Produce freely. Day-two rhythm (implementation, team heads-down on critical path): observe, wait, surface selectively. Produce when asked or when a load-bearing observation would disappear if not captured. Day-N (late, stable): naturalist contributes mostly at session transitions and when adversarial produces something new. The rhythm is not a productivity metric; it's a match between observation availability and team need. Over-producing on day two is a failure mode (Welford over-reach pattern). Under-producing on day one is also a failure mode (observations evaporate before they can be routed). Worth a standing methodology note for future sessions that spawn a naturalist role.
