## Initial Notes

Observation from my operating context (global CLAUDE.md): 'complexity can be right — don't flinch, investigate.' This trek is the clearest live example I've seen of that principle. RFA needs 52 bytes of state, five vector-state commutative monoid kinds (power sums / fintek 11-field MSR / Welford-LSE-Kahan / RFA / scalar phi-based Welford), three-axis tolerance, eleven invariants, three (now four or more) registries, compositional claims with three preconditions. None of this is simple; all of it is minimum-necessary. The trek's velocity comes from refusing to simplify when the complexity is load-bearing. Worth a standalone observation doc titled something like 'minimum necessary complexity at every layer of the trek' — a self-reinforcing reference the next session can cite when someone suggests simplifying I11 or collapsing the three tolerance axes or dropping a registry.
