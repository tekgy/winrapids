## Initial Notes

Follow-up to the P2-tightening audit in Entry 021: we ran the audit for NaN propagation. Do we need a similar audit for SIGNED ZERO propagation? IEEE 754 distinguishes +0 and -0, and the distinction matters in specific corners (division by zero, log(±0), atan2(±0, ±0), copysign). Adversarial's B1 on exp-design was exactly about signed zero: exp(-inf) must be +0, not -0. Every op in the capability matrix should be audited for: when this op produces zero, does it produce +0 or -0 per IEEE 754, and does our backend lowering preserve that? Question for future Vulkan/PTX implementers and for the capability matrix. Possibly becomes I12 if signed-zero propagation turns out to have its own set of cross-backend discrepancies — but don't name a new invariant until an actual counterexample forces it.
