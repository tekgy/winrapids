## Initial Notes

Reading list: Kahan's 'What Every Computer Scientist Should Know About Floating-Point Arithmetic' (Goldberg 1991) is the foundational reference on fp arithmetic corner cases. Every numerical analyst reads it once. It directly covers catastrophic cancellation, cancellation errors, the ULP concept, and the semantics of common pitfalls. The trek rediscovers several of its observations (variance catastrophic cancellation, FMA-vs-mul-add discrepancy). Worth a read for anyone on the team who hasn't seen it — especially math-researcher for the libm work, adversarial for the pitfall generation, and anyone writing ULP tolerance specs. URL: https://docs.oracle.com/cd/E19957-01/806-3568/ncg_goldberg.html is the canonical hosted version. Short paper (~40 pages). Naturalist did not re-read it this session and may have missed things that would have been obvious to someone who had.
