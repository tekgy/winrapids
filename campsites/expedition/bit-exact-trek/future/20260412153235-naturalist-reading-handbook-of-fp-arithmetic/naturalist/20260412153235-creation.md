## Initial Notes

Reading list: Muller et al., 'Handbook of Floating-Point Arithmetic' (Birkhauser, 2nd ed 2018). This is the comprehensive modern reference — Muller is the same Jean-Michel Muller who led CRlibm at Inria. Covers: IEEE 754 semantics in depth, rounding modes, correctly-rounded elementary functions (the Table Maker's Dilemma chapter is the canonical treatment), error analysis for compound operations, and verified numerical computation. If math-researcher's libm work wants to understand why CRlibm is hard and what it took to prove correctness, this is the book. ~600 pages — not a read-cover-to-cover, but specific chapters (the transcendentals chapter, the Table Maker's Dilemma chapter, the compound-error chapter) are directly relevant to Peak 2 and the ULP-composition pitfall journal entry. Possibly library-accessible; paywalled otherwise.
