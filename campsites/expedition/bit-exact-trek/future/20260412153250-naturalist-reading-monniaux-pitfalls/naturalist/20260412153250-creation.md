## Initial Notes

Reading list: David Monniaux, 'The pitfalls of verifying floating-point computations' (ACM TOPLAS 2008, arXiv cs/0701192). This paper is the definitive treatment of why formal verification of fp code is harder than people assume. Covers compiler transformations that break fp, the difference between fp-in-source and fp-on-hardware, why x86 80-bit extended-precision intermediates break portability assumptions, and specifically: WHY the kind of bit-exact-cross-backend guarantee tambear is targeting is hard. Directly relevant to I3/I4 (the no-FMA-contraction / no-implicit-reordering invariants) and to the P2 (faithful-lowering) precondition of the guarantee-ledger's compositional claim. Free on arXiv. ~30 pages. Worth math-researcher's time, and worth a read by whoever picks up Peak 3 (PTX assembler) or Peak 5 (CPU interpreter) because it names all the traps those layers historically fall into.
