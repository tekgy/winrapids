## Initial Notes

Reading: SLEEF (https://sleef.org, github.com/shibatch/sleef) as the reference for vectorized transcendental implementations. SLEEF is what Julia, Numpy, and Rust's num-traits recommend for fast-path vectorized math. Relevant to Peak 2 Phase 2+ when tambear wants vectorized libm paths. Key pattern to study: SLEEF's 'u1' tier (1 ULP) vs 'u35' tier (3.5 ULP) — both are vectorized, but they represent different trade-offs between polynomial degree and throughput. Tambear's Phase 1 is scalar and 1 ULP; Phase 2 may want vectorized. The SLEEF approach to branchless polynomial evaluation and vectorized argument reduction is the state of the art. Study is useful but SLEEF's code should NOT be ported directly (I1/I8 forbid).
