## Initial Notes

I7 specifies accumulate+gather (in that order) as the decomposition pair. Never asked: why accumulate-then-gather rather than gather-then-accumulate? What's the ordering mean in practice? (a) If the pair is 'accumulate from a source, then gather from accumulators,' the natural reading is that accumulate is the inner loop and gather is the outer. (b) If the pair is 'gather from source addresses, then accumulate the gathered values,' the order is flipped. (c) Some methods do BOTH: accumulate-gather-accumulate (e.g., distance matrix computation: accumulate squared differences, gather to form the matrix, accumulate row sums). Is the order intrinsic or arbitrary? Mini-deconstruction. Relates to whether accumulate+gather is sufficient or whether we need accumulate+gather+accumulate (three-step decomposition). Worth checking during the method-bias falsification test.
