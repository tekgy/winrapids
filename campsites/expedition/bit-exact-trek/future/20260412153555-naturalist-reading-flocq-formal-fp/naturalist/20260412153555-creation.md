Created: 2026-04-12T15:35:55-05:00

## Initial Notes

Reading list: Flocq (Melquiond et al., INRIA) is the Coq formalization library for IEEE 754 floating-point. Used in CompCert, Alt-Ergo, and many formal-verification projects. Key insight for tambear: Flocq's approach is to encode fp operations as functions over integer triples (sign, mantissa, exponent) and prove properties in Coq, rather than as f64 computations. The underlying model is the same one tambear implicitly uses when it reasons about bit-exactness. For anyone on the team who wants to know 'how would a formal verifier approach our P2 precondition (faithful-lowering),' Flocq is the reference point. Not immediately actionable, but worth knowing it exists. Paper: 'Flocq: A Unified Library for Proving Floating-Point Algorithms in Coq' (Boldo, Melquiond, ARITH 2011). URL: gitlab.inria.fr/flocq/flocq.
