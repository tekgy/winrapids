## Initial Notes

Reading/tool: Herbie (https://herbie.uwplse.org) is a tool from UW PLSE that takes an fp expression and automatically rewrites it for improved numerical accuracy. It uses a combination of error analysis, pattern matching, and search. Example: give it 'sqrt(x*x - y*y)' and it tells you the common-subexpression-elimination-breaking-cancellation failure mode and suggests 'sqrt((x-y)*(x+y))' as the alternative. Worth investigation for tambear-libm: (a) can Herbie audit our exp/log/sin/cos implementations for hidden accuracy issues, (b) can Herbie help generate adversarial inputs for the hard-cases suite by finding regions where our function diverges from mpmath most aggressively. Also Herbie's error-Taylor-series math is directly relevant to the ULP-composition pitfall I filed — their framework for tracking error propagation through compound expressions is probably more rigorous than the 'bounds compose additively' approximation I used.
