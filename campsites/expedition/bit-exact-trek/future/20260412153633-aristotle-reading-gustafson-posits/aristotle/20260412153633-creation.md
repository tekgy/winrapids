## Initial Notes

Reading thread. Gustafson's posit papers. I dismissed posits quickly in f64 deconstruction Phase 3 Option 6 as 'no hardware support, software emulation 10x-50x slower than fp64, bit-exact cross-hardware requires every ALU to agree on posit semantics which few do.' That dismissal may be right but it's based on received wisdom, not on reading. A fuller engagement would test whether the dismissal is sound. Specific questions: (a) are posits structurally EASIER to make bit-exact cross-hardware than IEEE-754 because the spec has fewer corner cases? (b) what does RISC-V's posit extension (if it exists in 2026) look like? (c) would a tambear backend for a posit-native accelerator (if such a thing emerges) be cleaner than the IEEE-754 backend path? If posits' dismissal is sound, this campsite confirms it. If posits' dismissal is wrong, this campsite produces a new deconstruction target.
