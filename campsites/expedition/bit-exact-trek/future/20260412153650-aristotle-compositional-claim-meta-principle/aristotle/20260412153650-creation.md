## Initial Notes

The trek's compositional claim (IR-precision + faithful-lowering + IEEE-754 compliance → bit-exact output) might generalize BEYOND tambear to any library claiming cross-hardware numerical correctness. The three preconditions are properties that can be stated for any library: (1) IR-precision = 'the library's source representation is precise enough to pin its behavior,' (2) faithful-lowering = 'every backend implements the source representation without introducing semantic drift,' (3) IEEE-754 compliance = 'the hardware implements the spec the library depends on.' Could be written as a meta-principle: 'Any library claiming cross-hardware correctness must satisfy these three conditions.' Not urgent. Publishable-as-a-software-engineering-paper territory. Post-trek work. Tekgy might be interested in the framing for other projects.
