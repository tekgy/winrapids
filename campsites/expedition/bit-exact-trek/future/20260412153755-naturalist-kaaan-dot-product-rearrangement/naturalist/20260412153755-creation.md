## Initial Notes

Observation: Kahan's 'dot product = sum of pairwise products' is subject to the same cancellation issues as variance. A naive dot product of (a, b) = sum(a_i * b_i) has worst-case O(n * eps) error. Compensated dot product (Kahan+Ogita+Rump, 2005) does the multiply in double-double and accumulates with compensated summation, giving O(eps) error independent of n. If tambear ever needs a dot-product primitive for Peak 6 or Peak 2's regression functions, this is the reference. Paper: Ogita, Rump, Oishi, 'Accurate sum and dot product' (SIAM J. Sci. Comput. 2005). The method is the same structure as Welford — a compensated accumulator — so its implementation would use the same pattern as Welford's n/mean/M2 tracking. Worth knowing exists; not needed for Phase 1.
