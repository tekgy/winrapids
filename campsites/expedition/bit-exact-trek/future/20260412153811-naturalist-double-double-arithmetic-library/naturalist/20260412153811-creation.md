## Initial Notes

Observation: double-double arithmetic (two f64 values representing one number with ~106 bits of precision) is a technique used by CRlibm and by most high-accuracy fp libraries. Classic implementations: qd (Bailey's QD library), Hida et al. 'Library for double-double and quad-double arithmetic' (2007). The operations are: add (Dekker / Knuth TwoSum), mul (Dekker / Ogita TwoProd), div, sqrt, reciprocal. Each is ~10 f64 ops. Relevant to tambear IF: (a) Phase 3 targets correctly-rounded libm, (b) the guarantee-ledger's P1 precondition ever wants to support extended precision as a first-class primitive, (c) PCA / covariance / regression ever needs more precision than f64 can provide. The algorithms are well-known but not simple — a full double-double library would be a ~1000-line addition to tambear. Not needed Phase 1, but worth knowing exists.
