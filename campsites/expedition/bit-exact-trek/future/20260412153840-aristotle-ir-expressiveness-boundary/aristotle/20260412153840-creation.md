## Initial Notes

The .tam IR has ~20 ops as of Peak 1 completion (arithmetic + comparison + select + loop_grid_stride + reduce_block_add + libm stubs + integer ops + bitcast + ldexp + f64_to_i32_rn). Is this the COMPLETE set of ops needed to express Phase 1 recipes bit-exactly? Or are there gaps? Specifically: are there any mathematical operations that the recipes USE (even through libraries) that the IR cannot express primitively? Candidate gaps: integer division (intentionally omitted per Peak 1), floating-point division with specific rounding modes, NaN-aware min/max beyond I11 (fmin/fmax in IEEE-754-2019 have richer NaN semantics than I11 specifies), classification ops (isnan, isinf, isfinite — are these in the current IR?), conversion ops between precisions (relevant to f64 Phase 2). Mini-audit: enumerate all ops used in the 26 fintek recipes at the math level, cross-reference against the Peak 1 IR op list, flag gaps. Produces either (a) an empty gap list (IR is complete for Phase 1) or (b) a concrete list of missing ops for Peak 2 discussion.
