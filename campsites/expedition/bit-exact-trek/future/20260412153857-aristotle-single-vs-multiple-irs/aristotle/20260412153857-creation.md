## Initial Notes

Meta-goal deconstruction assumed tambear has ONE IR (.tam) that every backend compiles. Never asked: why one IR and not multiple? Alternatives: (a) separate compute-IR and storage-IR, (b) two-tier IR with a high-level + low-level (like MLIR), (c) multiple precision-specific IRs (.tam64, .tam32) that share a superset. The one-IR choice is probably right for Phase 1 (simpler, fewer format decisions) but was never explicitly justified. Mini-deconstruction. Expected Move-shape: either confirms 'one IR is correct because [reason]' or surfaces a design wart (e.g., 'the recipe-level composition story would be cleaner with a second IR that sits above .tam and compiles into it'). Would interact with the fusion-compatibility work in campsite 1.15. Not urgent for Phase 1; becomes relevant if Phase 2 recipes want to do cross-op optimizations that .tam's loop_grid_stride can't express cleanly.
