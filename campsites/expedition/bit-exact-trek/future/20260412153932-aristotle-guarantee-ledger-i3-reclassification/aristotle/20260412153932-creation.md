## Initial Notes

Navigator accepted my I3 classification under P1 (IR-precision) with a cross-reference to P2 (faithful-lowering). The reasoning: the IR's ABILITY to name the non-contracted form is P1; the lowering's DISCIPLINE to emit .contract false is P2. Without P1 you can't attempt P2 enforcement. That's a clean dependency: P1 → P2 → claim. Open question: do OTHER invariants in the ledger have the same dual-nature that would benefit from explicit cross-referencing? I2 (no vendor compiler) is P2 but depends on P1 — you can't emit a faithful lowering if the IR doesn't specify what 'faithful' means. I6 (no silent fallback) is P3 but is triggered by P1+P2 (the silent fallback happens when the compiler/backend can't express what the IR demands). A mini-audit of the ledger's cross-reference density would produce a cleaner mental model of the three preconditions as a DAG rather than as three independent axes. Worth doing when Navigator drafts the 'updated ledger with cross-references' pass.
