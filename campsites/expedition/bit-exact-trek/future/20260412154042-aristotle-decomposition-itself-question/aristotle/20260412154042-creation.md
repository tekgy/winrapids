## Initial Notes

Deepest unasked question of the session. I7 is 'every primitive decomposes into accumulate+gather.' The decomposition assumption was questioned (Phase 8 forced rejection: what if there's no decomposition at all?) and found to be a SPEED story, not a CORRECTNESS story. But the deeper version wasn't asked: what if the TWO-STEP decomposition (accumulate + gather) is itself the wrong level of granularity? Alternatives: (a) one-step — everything is a single 'scan' op with a rich parameter bag; (b) three-step — accumulate + gather + emit, making the I/O side first-class; (c) N-step — the IR is a DAG of specific primitives (add, mul, load, store) with no decomposition-level scaffolding at all, just the DAG. Each alternative has different implications for the method (what gets fused, what can be shared, what backends must implement). Never deconstructed. Potentially the most interesting single Aristotle target because it would audit the premise the entire trek rests on. Risk: if the answer is 'two-step is optimal,' it's a big investment for a confirmation. Reward: if the answer is 'the right decomposition is N-step and we've been wrong,' the trek gets rearchitected. High variance.
