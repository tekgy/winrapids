## Initial Notes

Team-lead's example slug from the session-end broadcast was 'aristotle-decomp-what-if-ternary-ops-were-primary'. I'm taking that as a planted seed. The thought experiment: the trek's IR is built around binary ops (fadd, fsub, fmul, fdiv). Ternary ops like FMA are considered 'compositions' of binary. What if the IR's primitive unit was TERNARY? Obvious benefits: FMA is common in modern hardware; many algorithms want the fused form; three operands match natural 'read two sources and a destination' ISA patterns. Obvious costs: the set of ternary ops is larger than binary (more to enumerate, more to lower, more to prove correctness for); I3's 'no FMA contraction' becomes meaningless because FMA is the primitive, not a contraction. Third-order effect: ternary primitives change what gets fused in Peak 6's accumulator design (RFA's inner loop is naturally ternary). Worth a small deconstruction. Could upset the current IR design but also might produce nothing. Team-lead pre-seeded this so they probably want it explored.
