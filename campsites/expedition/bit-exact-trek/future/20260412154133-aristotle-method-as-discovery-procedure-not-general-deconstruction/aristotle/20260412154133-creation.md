## Initial Notes

Sharper restatement of the convergence-check finding that deserves its own campsite. I used to describe Aristotle as 'first-principles deconstruction' — a general process for questioning assumptions. The convergence check showed that as-practiced this session, the method is actually MORE SPECIFIC: it's a discovery procedure for one class of finding (convention-to-declaration, subject to fragility threshold). That's not a weakness; it's clarity about what the method is FOR. The trek's early-Phase-1 state has many implicit conventions to find and promote, so the method is productive. A later phase with different work might need a different method. Worth thinking about: is there a SECOND Aristotle method that would be productive on different targets? (Example: a 'dual-purpose audit' that finds where two concerns — speed and correctness, say — are being served by one artifact and might benefit from separation.) If so, building the second method would expand Aristotle's toolkit beyond the one-class discovery procedure I now know the method is.
