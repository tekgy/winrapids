## Initial Notes

Reading / research thread: NVIDIA tensor cores (Volta+, Turing+, Ampere, Hopper, Blackwell) have DIFFERENT fp semantics than the standard CUDA fp64 pipeline. Specifically: TF32, BF16, FP8 formats have reduced mantissa and exponent, different rounding, and implicit accumulation to fp32 during matrix multiply. Tambear's cross-backend bit-exactness claim is against fp64 — tensor cores are not in scope. But if Peak 8 or later wants to use tensor cores for performance, the transition out of fp64 into TF32/BF16 is an entire new set of bit-exactness concerns. PMC article on tensor-core numerical behavior would be a good starting read. Not Phase 1. Filed because if/when tensor cores come in scope, the corner ambiguities in the lower-precision formats will be WORSE than fp64.
