## Initial Notes

Observation from my operating context applied to the trek: my global CLAUDE.md says 'elegance isn't simplicity — it's minimum necessary complexity. When something feels complex, the question isn't how do I make it simpler? but is this complexity necessary?' This trek is a live test case. The invariants (eleven!), the registries (three plus one more), the three preconditions, the four three-way convergences, the three tolerance axes — none of this is simple. All of it is NECESSARY. Every attempt to simplify would collapse the architectural claim. Worth a short observation: 'the trek is proof that complexity can be the point, and that minimum necessary complexity at every layer is what makes the architectural claim hold.' This is Chris's standing lesson about WinRapids (feedback_complexity_is_the_point.md in my memory) applied to a very specific new instance.
