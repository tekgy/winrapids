## Initial Notes

Meta-observation: session transitions are where knowledge is most likely to be lost AND most likely to be consolidated. Team-lead's shutdown broadcast forced every role to write down what was in-context and file pickup points. That single broadcast generated: 40+ naturalist campsites (in one session), aristotle's synthesis.md and deferred-candidates.md, the guarantee-ledger, the practice file exemplar finding, navigator's check-ins updates, multiple garden entries across roles. Session transitions are actually PRODUCTIVITY EVENTS if structured properly. The opposite of what you'd expect. Worth a team-process observation: 'session-seam rituals as consolidation events.' Future sessions should build in structured shutdown broadcasts at predictable intervals, not just when literal machine transfers happen. Possibly: mid-session 'garden transition' broadcasts too, as the practice file's garden-boundary rule suggests.
