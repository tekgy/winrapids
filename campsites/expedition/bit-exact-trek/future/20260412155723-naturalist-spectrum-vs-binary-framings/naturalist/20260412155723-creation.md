## Initial Notes

Pattern observation: many of the trek's refinements took the shape 'what looked binary is actually a spectrum.' Examples: (1) bit-exact vs bounded-ULP turned out to be three axes (Entry 020). (2) Correctness vs speed was revealed to be two orthogonal axes not one (aristotle I7 refinement). (3) Convention vs declaration turned out to be a tuning knob, not a switch (practice file exemplar finding). (4) Bucket A (use vendor op) vs Bucket B (compose from primitives) was about which IEEE-754 layer the op touches, not good-vs-bad. In every case, the binary framing was a first approximation that the team refined into a spectrum or multi-axis structure. Worth naming: 'binary framings are first approximations; look for the spectrum or the orthogonal axis.' Could be a standing methodology note for aristotle or naturalist.
