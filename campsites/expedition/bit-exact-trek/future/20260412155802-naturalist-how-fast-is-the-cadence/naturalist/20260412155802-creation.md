## Initial Notes

Numerical observation on team pace: I generated roughly 50 campsites in one focused shutdown window after team-lead's 'volume is the goal' emphasis. Most of them are one-paragraph gifts rather than designed artifacts. The speed suggests campsites are ~90 seconds of thought each for ideas that were already in working memory. Implication for future team design: a role doing active observation work should have regular 'campsite flush' windows where they dump working-memory observations into the logbook without curation. 10 campsites per hour is achievable. 50 campsites before shutdown is achievable. The limiting resource is not thinking; it's the willingness to bias toward filing over curation. The shift from 'be precise about each' to 'spray seed packets' is a mental-model change, not a capability change.
