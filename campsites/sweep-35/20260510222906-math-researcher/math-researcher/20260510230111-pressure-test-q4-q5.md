## Pressure-test #1 — Q5 sub-case collapse: 6 → 3?

**Aristotle's prediction**: the six (F, G) instantiations I named are sub-cases of three deeper classes:

| Class | Constituents |
|-------|--------------|
| Additive-fixed-point | (0, additive) — expm1, cosm1, sinh-near-0, tanh-near-0 |
| Multiplicative-fixed-point | (1, multiplicative) — log1p; (2^ℤ, multiplicative) — exp2-integer; (e^ℤ, multiplicative) — exp/log-Tang; (π·ℤ, additive-period-π) — sinpi/cospi; (90·ℤ, additive-period-90) — sind/cosd |
| Scaling-group-on-submanifold | (unit-circle, scaling-on-ℝ²) — hypot, cabs |

If the collapse is clean, naturalist's three-shapes IS the underlying framework with (F, G) as a quotient/parameterization within Shapes 1+2. If the collapse breaks somewhere, three-shapes is incomplete.

### Walking the test

**Subclass A: Additive-fixed-point** — F is a single point in R; G acts additively on R near F.
- `expm1(x)`: F = 0 (in output space); G = additive translation on output; **but wait** — past-naturalist's three-shapes framing says expm1 is Shape 2 (output-side transform), and Shape 2's (F, G) acts on the *codomain*, not the domain. The "additive-fixed-point" framing conflates domain and codomain action.

**This is the first crack**: aristotle's three-class prediction treats (F, G) as acting in one space; my original walk treated different members as acting on different spaces (input-side for sinpi, output-side for expm1). The unification doesn't collapse cleanly without naming WHICH space the (F, G) action lives in.

**Refined collapse attempt**:

| Refined class | Constituents | Action on |
|---------------|--------------|-----------|
| Codomain-additive-near-0 | expm1, cosm1 | output |
| Codomain-additive-near-1 | log1p (via `log(1+x) = ?` — actually it's domain-side: input is `1+ε`, the "1+" is the domain transform) | (mixed) |
| Domain-multiplicative-near-integer-grid | sinpi, cospi, tanpi, sind, cosd, tand | input |
| Domain-multiplicative-via-Tang | exp/log/exp2/log2/exp10/log10 (k·ln(2)+r reduction) | input |
| Domain-exact-via-bitwise | exp2-integer, log2-power-of-2 (frexp/ldexp) | input |
| Scaling-on-submanifold | hypot, cabs (no F, G in the same sense; algorithm IS the rewrite) | (different shape) |

Six classes, not three. The collapse aristotle predicted **doesn't happen at the same axis** he proposed. The deeper structure (input-vs-output, plus the action group) splits the six (F, G) into more classes than three, not fewer.

**But**: this is *also* not aristotle being wrong. It's evidence that **the (F, G) parameterization is incomplete on its own**. Naming (F, G) without naming WHICH-SPACE-it-acts-in produces apparent unification that breaks under closer examination. The fix is naming the axis explicitly: every (F, G) instantiation needs three coordinates — (F, G, space-of-action).

### Finding A1: (F, G) needs a third coordinate

The meta-primitive is parameterized by **(F, G, σ)** where σ ∈ {domain, codomain, mixed}. The April 13 garden's framing dropped σ implicitly. The six instantiations I named in Q5 have implicit σ values:

| Instantiation | (F, G, σ) |
|---------------|-----------|
| expm1, cosm1 | (0, additive, codomain) |
| log1p | (1, multiplicative, domain) [or: (0, additive, "output-of-log-becomes-input-of-1-plus-result")] |
| sinpi, cospi, tanpi | (π·ℤ, additive-period-π, domain-via-scaling-by-π) |
| sind, cosd, tand | (90·ℤ, additive-period-90, domain-via-scaling-by-π/180) |
| exp2-integer, log2-power-of-2 | (2^ℤ, multiplicative, domain-via-frexp) |
| exp/log/Tang | (e^ℤ, multiplicative, domain-via-k·ln(2)+r) |

Plus Shape 3 instantiations that don't fit the (F, G, σ) framework at all:
- hypot, cabs, norm — submanifold-fixed-point with scaling-action; σ is irrelevant because the algorithm doesn't separate transform / core / inverse (per naturalist's Shape 3).
- asin-near-1, atanh-near-1 — recursive-identity; σ would be "domain-via-half-angle" but the recursion makes σ change between recursive levels.

### Finding A2: Sub-case collapse partially holds but adds a coordinate

Aristotle's three-class prediction (additive / multiplicative / scaling) collapses correctly along the *G axis* alone:
- All six "kingdom I/II" instantiations have G ∈ {additive, multiplicative}.
- The Shape-3 instantiations have G = scaling-on-submanifold.

The G axis alone has 3 classes (additive, multiplicative, scaling). My original 6-row table conflated G with F + σ. **Aristotle is right that the deeper class is 3-valued, but the deeper class is G alone — not (F, G).** F is a within-class parameter (multiple F values within "multiplicative"); σ is an orthogonal coordinate.

**Lifted framework**: the meta-primitive has **3 group-class axes × (F-within-class) × σ ∈ {domain, codomain}**. Six instantiations from Q5 are 6 cells in this 3D space; the periodic-table-revisited doc's "8 reductions" are 8 (G, F, σ) points; the difference is degenerate cells where (G, F, σ) doesn't pick a valid σ (Shape-3 cases).

### Finding A3: Three-shapes-vs-G-axis identity question

Naturalist's three-shapes (Shape 1 / Shape 2 / Shape 3) at first glance overlaps with the G-axis (additive / multiplicative / scaling). Is the overlap exact?

- Shape 1 (input-side transform) → G = multiplicative (because input is `(1+ε)` or `π·x` — both are multiplicative actions on the input)
- Shape 2 (output-side transform) → G = additive (because output is `result - F` for F a single point in codomain)
- Shape 3 (structural rewrite) → G = scaling (or no G at all if Shape 3 admits arbitrary algorithms)

**Test against the actual data**:
- expm1, cosm1 → Shape 2, G = additive ✓
- log1p → ??? Shape 1 (input-side) but G = multiplicative? The naming "log1p" suggests output-of-log-then-input-of-log-near-1. The (F, G, σ) framing says (1, multiplicative, domain). Naturalist's Shape 1 says input-side. These agree.
- sinpi → Shape 1, G = multiplicative-on-domain ✓
- exp/log/Tang reduction → Shape 1 (input is k·ln(2)+r, the k·ln(2) is the reduction transform on the domain), G = multiplicative-on-domain (additive in r-coord after the transform, but the transform itself is multiplicative)? Or G = additive (because k·ln(2) is added/subtracted)?

**The G axis breaks down here**. exp's Tang reduction is *additive-in-r* (`r = x - k·ln(2)`) but *multiplicative-as-reduction* (`exp(x) = 2^k · exp(r)`). Both views are valid; which one is "the G" depends on whether you look at the reduction step (additive) or the reconstruction step (multiplicative).

### Finding A4: The G-axis is NOT a clean invariant

The G-axis depends on whether you describe the (F, G, σ) at the reduction step or at the reconstruction step. For most members (sinpi: reduction = multiply by π = multiplicative; reconstruction = identity), these agree. For Tang-reduction-based functions (exp/log family), they DISAGREE — reduction-G is additive, reconstruction-G is multiplicative.

**This is a real finding**, not an error in my walk. It says: the (F, G, σ) framework has an additional implicit axis I didn't name — *which-step-of-the-pipeline G describes*. Naming this fourth axis as **τ ∈ {reduction-step, reconstruction-step, both}** gives the framework five coordinates: (F, G, σ, τ, action-space). At which point we're past parsimonious abstraction.

**My position**: stop adding coordinates. The (F, G) framing is **descriptive**, not **structural**. It's useful vocabulary for naming families in the literature; it's NOT a clean classification axis for the recipe-tier metadata. Three-shapes (Shape 1/2/3) IS structural (because it's defined by where the recipe puts the fix, not by an abstract group-theoretic property of the function). **Aristotle's sub-case collapse hypothesis was right that the 6 instantiations are over-counted, but the right collapse is not 6 → 3 along (F, G, σ); it's 6 → integration-into-three-shapes-which-is-already-the-structural-axis.**

### Verdict on Pressure-test #1

**Q5's "six (F, G) instantiations" is descriptive substrate, not a structural classification.** The six instantiations are useful names for cells in the periodic-table-revisited doc's tagged-graph; they're NOT the same level of abstraction as kingdom-membership or three-shapes. Aristotle's pressure-test correctly surfaced that they over-count by treating G as the primary axis when σ (action-space) and τ (which-step) are co-axes with G.

**The lift**: when the periodic-table-revisited doc's `natural_reduction: ReductionFG` field eventually ships in the recipe metadata, it should be a **named-tag enum** (sinpi-reduction, Tang-reduction, frexp-reduction, hypot-scaling, ...) **not** a (F, G) tuple. The naming is what makes the family checkable per past-scout's April 10 entry. The (F, G) parameterization is a *secondary* descriptive metadata that's useful for documentation, not for codegen dispatch.

**Concrete edit needed**: periodic-table-revisited doc § "Implications for the recipe metadata schema" should rename `natural_reduction: ReductionFG` to `natural_reduction: ReductionTag` where ReductionTag is a named-variant enum, not a (F, G) tuple. Going to make this edit in next routing pass.

## Pressure-test #2 — Three uncomfortable Q4 cells

### Cell #1: `pow(integer-base, half-integer-exponent)`

**The discomfort**: my Q1 walk placed pow as Kingdom III (composite-kernel-state). But `pow(4, 0.5) = 2` is exact at f64; `pow(2, 1.5) = 2·√2` is sqrt-exact-times-2. These shouldn't go through `exp(0.5 · log(4))` — they should hit a special-case path that detects the integer-base + half-integer-exponent pattern and dispatches to sqrt.

**Mini Phase 1: where exactly is the cell empty?**

The cell is empty in my Q1 walk because the composite-form description (`pow = exp ∘ log` composition) doesn't account for special-case detection. The math exists (well-defined product); the literature names it (IEEE 754 mandates exactness for `pow(x, 0.5) = sqrt(x)` for non-negative x); but the recipe-tier *classification* doesn't have a slot for "Kingdom III composite recipe with bypass-to-special-case-detection."

**Mini Phase 2: structural reason for emptiness**

The Kingdom III description treats pow as a single composition path. But pow is actually a **dispatcher**:
- `pow(x, 0) = 1` — exact, no composition
- `pow(x, 1) = x` — exact, no composition
- `pow(x, 0.5) = sqrt(x)` for non-negative x — exact via sqrt, NOT composition
- `pow(x, integer n)` — exact via binary exponentiation (squaring), NOT composition
- `pow(x, half-integer = m + 0.5)` — exact via `pow(x, m) · sqrt(x)`, NOT composition
- `pow(x, general y)` — composition via `exp(y · log(x))`

So pow is **not** Kingdom III in my classification — it's a **Kingdom Mixed** with a special-case dispatcher and a fallback to Kingdom III. The recipe metadata schema needs to admit this hybrid.

**Mini Phase 3: structural finding**

**The Kingdom taxonomy is missing "dispatcher recipes."** A dispatcher recipe has a regime-detection step at the top that routes to one of several sub-recipes; the sub-recipes themselves are in different Kingdoms. pow is the canonical example. So is `cbrt` (special-case for power-of-8 inputs + general path). So is `tanh` for very large |x| (saturates to ±1) vs general (uses ExpKernelState).

**Lift**: add **Kingdom V: Dispatcher recipes** to the classification, with the property that a dispatcher's *children* are in other kingdoms. The dispatcher's metadata is just (a) the dispatch predicate set, (b) the sub-recipe targets.

This was an *empty* cell in my Q4 sketch. The empty cell IS the load-bearing finding. There are at least three dispatcher recipes in the libm catalog (pow, cbrt, tanh-or-similar-saturating) that need this Kingdom V slot.

### Cell #2: `log(exp(x))` round-trip — recipe or test?

**The discomfort**: my K-S-C-T (kernel-state-consistency-tests) doc § 6 lists `log(exp(x)) ≈ x` as a Pattern-B round-trip identity for the proptest harness. But is "the log-of-exp" *itself* a recipe? The identity says the composition equals the identity function — should there be a `identity_recipe` slot for compositions-that-equal-identity?

**Mini Phase 1**

The cell would be the entry for "compositions of recipes that equal a primitive operation (identity, sqrt, log, etc.)." It's empty in my Q4 sketch because compositions aren't first-class catalog entries. But mathematically, log-of-exp == identity is a *theorem* about the recipes, not a recipe itself.

**Mini Phase 2**

The structural reason this cell is empty: **recipes are functions; theorems about recipes are tests.** The composition `log(exp(x))` evaluates to x mathematically, but as a *recipe* it would compute `log(exp(x))` numerically and could drift from x by ≤2 ulps. If the drift is 0, that's a stronger condition than the math requires — it's a consistency invariant about the implementations.

So the cell isn't a recipe. The cell is a *test*. Specifically, it's a kernel-state-consistency-test (per K-S-C-T doc Pattern B). The taxonomy is fine; recipes vs tests are distinct.

**But there's a subtler discomfort**: what about `log10(10^x) = x`? This is an identity for the recipe-pair (log10, exp10). Is the harness ALSO supposed to generate this as a test? And `log2(2^x) = x`? At some point, every recipe-pair that's mathematically inverse generates a test.

**Mini Phase 3: structural finding**

**Recipe-pair tests are generated, not enumerated.** For every (forward, inverse) recipe pair, the round-trip identity is a generated test. This is a *test-generation rule*, not a cell in the recipe table. The recipe-tier metadata should include an `inverse_of: Option<RecipeId>` field; when present, the proptest harness auto-generates the round-trip test.

This is a *non-finding* for the recipe table (no missing cell) but a *positive finding* for the harness: the metadata should expose inverse-recipe relationships so the harness can generate round-trip tests automatically. Adds one field to the schema.

### Cell #3: `atanh(x)` near ±1 — Shape 2 or Shape 3?

**The discomfort**: `atanh(x) = (1/2) · log((1+x)/(1-x))`. Reading 1: this is Shape 2 (output-side transform of log). Reading 2: near x = ±1, the (1-x) term causes catastrophic cancellation; the fix is a Shape 3 structural rewrite using `log1p((2x)/(1-x))` or similar. So is atanh Shape 2 or Shape 3?

**Mini Phase 1**

The cell is "atanh near ±1." It's empty in my Q4 sketch because Shape 2 vs Shape 3 isn't a within-recipe distinction; a recipe has *one* shape position. atanh-near-±1 sits ambiguously.

**Mini Phase 2**

The structural reason: **a recipe can have multiple shape positions depending on input regime.** atanh in `[-0.5, 0.5]` is Shape 2 (output of log-of-ratio, no cancellation in (1-x)). atanh in `[0.9, 1)` is Shape 3 (the cancellation forces a structural rewrite using log1p). The *recipe* atanh has ONE name but TWO shape positions across input regimes.

This matches naturalist's second addendum: *"shapes are coordinates, not categories; a function's recipe is a path through shape-space."* atanh's path is: Shape 2 in the middle of the domain, Shape 3 near the boundaries. The recipe's *shape position* metadata is a *map* (input-regime → shape-position), not a single discriminant.

**Mini Phase 3: structural finding**

**Shape-position metadata is regime-dependent.** The recipe metadata schema (per periodic-table-revisited doc) has a `shape_position: ThreeShapesCoord` field; this should be `shape_position: Map<InputRegime, ThreeShapesCoord>` (or equivalently, an `Option<ThreeShapesCoord>` with a separate `regime_to_shape: Vec<(RegimeRange, ThreeShapesCoord)>` for the dispatcher case).

Same finding shape as Cell #1 (dispatcher recipes): the recipe metadata schema needs to admit **regime-dependent variation in classification fields.** This is naturalist's "shapes are coordinates" lifted to the metadata level.

### Verdict on Pressure-test #2

Three uncomfortable cells, three findings:
1. **Kingdom V (Dispatcher recipes)** needs to be added — pow, cbrt, regime-dispatching tanh, others.
2. **Inverse-recipe pairs** need an `inverse_of: Option<RecipeId>` field — enables auto-generated round-trip tests.
3. **Shape-position is regime-dependent** — needs a `regime_to_shape: Vec<(RegimeRange, ThreeShapesCoord)>` map, not a single discriminant.

All three findings strengthen the schema. None contradict the kingdom-genealogy framing. The empty-cell-as-information framing aristotle predicted held perfectly — each empty cell pointed to a missing axis in the metadata schema, not noise to ignore.

## Pressure-test #3 — Synthesis

### What survived

- **Kingdom-genealogy framing** (periodic-table-revisited): survives. The kingdom-and-(F,G)-genealogy is the right shape; the cells inside are tagged by kingdom + reduction-name (not by (F, G, σ, τ, ...) tuple).
- **Three-shapes framing** (naturalist's): survives. Three-shapes IS the structural axis; (F, G) is descriptive substrate, not a structural classification.
- **Four-axis recipe schema** (naturalist's four axes): survives, with refinements:
  - axis-2 (fix-shape) needs to be a regime-map, not a single discriminant.
  - Add axis-5: kingdom (with Kingdom V for dispatchers) — kingdom is a *derived* coordinate from the other four, NOT a fifth axis. (Per my periodic-table-revisited conjecture: `|kernel_state_deps| + content-addressability → kingdom`.) Kingdom V (dispatcher) needs its own derivation rule.
  - Add `inverse_of: Option<RecipeId>` field for auto-generated round-trip tests.
- **Representation-precision-matching invariant**: survives unchanged. Independent finding from the pressure-test.

### What was wrong in my original walks

- **Q5's "six (F, G) instantiations" as structural axis**: wrong. They're descriptive substrate; the structural axis is three-shapes + regime-map.
- **periodic-table-revisited's `natural_reduction: ReductionFG`**: needs renaming to `natural_reduction: ReductionTag` (named enum, not (F, G) tuple). Going to edit in next pass.
- **Q4's implicit assumption that recipes have a single shape position**: wrong (atanh is the counter-example). Shape position is regime-dependent.
- **Q4's missing Kingdom V (dispatcher recipes)**: pow, cbrt, regime-dispatching tanh need this slot. Three recipes I had been silently treating as Kingdom III are actually dispatchers with Kingdom III as one of their children.

### Methodological reflection

Aristotle's Phase 6 methodology + the "empty cells as load-bearing finding" reframe were both substantively productive. The pressure-test surfaced three concrete schema corrections that I would NOT have found by continuing to write more lifts of the existing framing — they required *adversarial deconstruction* of my own walks, applied to specific uncomfortable cells.

**The strongest finding methodologically**: aristotle's prediction (six → three sub-cases) was *false in the form he predicted* (the sub-case axis isn't (F, G, σ); it's three-shapes + (G-as-secondary)) but *correct in the structural claim it pointed at* (the six instantiations are over-counted at the structural axis level). The Phase 6 methodology works *not* by predicting the right collapse, but by *predicting a collapse exists* and forcing the walker to look. The collapse that emerged is different from the predicted one, but the *existence* of the collapse was the load-bearing prediction.

This is the same shape as F13 OQ#6's site-convergence claim: convergence isn't on identical specifics, it's on the underlying *structural existence* of the constraint. Aristotle predicted a 6→3 collapse; the walk surfaced a 6→2 (descriptive vs structural) collapse instead. Different specifics, same structural claim that the original count was over-counted.
